About Me

I'm a Research Assistant at the Institute for Electromagnetic Sensing of the Environment (IREA), National Research Council of Italy (CNR) in Naples, Italy. My research focuses on remote sensing with active and passive sensors, particularly the development of InSAR algorithms for monitoring surface deformation phenomena.

I received my Ph.D. in Engineering for Innovation and Sustainable Development from the University of Basilicata in 2023. In the same year, I had the opportunity to work as a Visiting Scientist at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), Caltech.

My expertise includes SAR data processing, machine learning applications in remote sensing, and the development of algorithms for analyzing areas subject to ground deformation.
